1. Defying Definition: Why Jet Engines Don't Melt

It's an engineering paradox: The inside of a modern jet engine operates at temperatures comfortably above the melting point of the metals it's made of. How is this possible?

In a recent deep dive by Veritasium, the secrets of turbine blade cooling were revealed. The key lies in a combination of advanced materials and ingenious cooling strategies.

  • Ceramic Coatings: Blades are coated in thermal barrier ceramics that resist extreme heat.
  • Internal Cooling Channels: Complex pathways inside the blade allow cooler air from the compressor to circulate, stripping away heat.
  • Film Cooling: Tiny holes on the blade surface create a thin "film" of cool air that insulates the metal from the hot combustion gases.

This is a masterclass in thermodynamics.
